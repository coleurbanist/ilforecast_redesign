## Sources
Election results from Illinois State Board of Elections (https://www.elections.il.gov/electionoperations/ElectionVoteTotalsPrecinct.aspx?ID=9huvqbsiUWA%3d). Please note that non-precinct level votes have been allocated to precincts based on each precincts' share of precinct-level votes for that contest within the relevant reporting unit.

Precinct boundaries for Illinois' 102 counties come from a variety of sources:

## Notes on Field Names:
Columns reporting votes generally follow the pattern:
One example is:
GPREDCLI
The first character is G for a general election, P for a primary, S for a special, and R for a runoff.
Characters 2 and 3 are the year of the election.
Characters 4-6 represent the office type (see list below).
Character 7 represents the party of the candidate.
Characters 8-10 are the first three letters of the candidate's last name.

Office Codes Used:
BI# - Ballot Initiative
PRE - President of the US
CON## - U.S. Congress
SC# - Supreme Court Vacancy
SL### - Illinois House of Representatives
SU## - Illinois Senate

Party Codes Used:
D - Democratic
I - Independent
N - Non-Partisan
O - Other
R - Republican
W - Working Class Party

## Processing Steps
In many counties, Illinois reported election results by ballot type, which can be smaller than the precinct-level in cases in which precincts are split by districts. When it was clear from election results that precincts were split by Congressional, Illinois Senate, or Illinois General Assembly districts, the precinct shapes were split using these boundaries in order to maintain the granularity of the election data available from the state.

In order to join the election results to precinct boundaries, federal-only ballots, which were reported at the precinct level (and kept separate in the election results file) were combined with the relevant ballot type in that precinct. In the rare instances where a precinct contained multiple ballot types and the federal-only ballot did not have a clear district identifier, these votes were combined with the ballot type in that precinct with the most votes. 

After joining the precincts to their boundaries, the files were checked for precinct-district splits. Ultimately, the precinct boundary and election result files are split across four different files. The "_all_" file contains all the election results joined to precinct boundaries, but does not account for the various precinct-district splits identified in processing and as such, does not contain district assignments. The "_cong_", "_sldl_", and "_sldu_" files contain Congressional, General Assembly and Illinois Senate results at the most granular units for which data is available. Please note that in order to minimize superfluous splits, precinct splits are only made when a precinct contains votes for candidates in multiple districts, as such district assignments for precincts many not perfectly correspond to the districts themselves, particularly in large, uninhabited areas.

In a handful of cases, votes appear in contests in which no portion of the precinct is contained within the district. This occurred in 1 precinct (COOK-:-8700038) in 1 Congressional district, 1 precinct (MCLEAN-:-NORMAL 12) in 1 Illinois Senate District and 8 precincts (MACON-:-MAROA, ST. CLAIR-:-O FALLON 5, MACON-:-AUSTIN, ST. CLAIR-:-CASEYVILLE 8, WINNEBAGO-:-ROCKFORD 8, TAZEWELL-:-CINCINNATI 3, MCLEAN-:-NORMAL 12, and TAZEWELL-:-GROVELAND 6) across 7 general assembly districts. As a result of this issue, these votes are removed and totals will not exactly match in the "_sldl_", "_cong_" and "_sldu_" version of the files. None of these votes are not removed from the "_all_" file.

Precinct-level results were checked against county-level results from the Illinois State Board of Elections and found to match in all instances.
